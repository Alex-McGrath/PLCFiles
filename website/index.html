What is Mountain Biking?

The dictionary definition of Mountain Biking is "A sport of riding bicycles off road, often over rough terrain, usually using specically designed mountain bikes" but just like any other sport, the definition may vary from person to person. For example, professionals and hardcore Mountain bikers would even go as far as saying its a lifestyle. Types of Mountain Biking

There are many different types of Mountain Biking: 


            

            
            
            

                Cross Country (XC)
            

            

            

                Cross country is a style of mountain biking, probably the most well known due to its appearances at
                the Olympics. It's all about fitness and stamina, with cross-country race courses involving steep 
                climbs and technical descents with features such as rock-gardens, rollers and drops. The courses 
                arenâ€™t as technical as downhill tracks, but riders have to exert maximum effort for the entire 
                duration of the race. XC bikes are commonly hardtail with 130mm suspension forks but full suspension
                are increasing in popularity. The bikes are designed to be as light and effcient.
                

            

            

            
            
Downhill (DH)

            

            

                Downhill Mountain Biking is fairly self explanatory, you start the track at the top of the mountain
                and race down to the bottom, the person who has the fastest time down is the winner. Riders can reach
                up to speeds of 80kph and hit jumps up to 19m long. Downhill bikes are designed specifically for riding
                down the steep tracks. They are full suspension with 200mm of travel in the fork and an aggresive rear shock.
                

            

            

            
            
Freeride

            

            

                Freeride mountain biking is a variant of downhill riding where riders perform huge jumps, flips and tricks while 
                riding on natural terrain. A common location for freeride competitions is the Zion National Park in Utah, USA.
                To win a freeride competition you need to be creative with your riding, usually the riders can choose their own way 
                down. The bikes that freeriders use would be the exact same spec as the downhill bikes but with the suspension 
                pumped up a little harder.
                

            

            

            
            
Slopestyle

            

            

                Slopestyle, closely related to the tricks part of freeride, slopestyle is all about showcasing aerial tricks on courses
                specically designed with huge jumps, drops and berms. The courses closely resemble a scaled up skatepark, the rider with
                the best and most technical tricks has the highest chance of winning. The bikes these riders use are smaller than most other
                styles, theyre light, small, strong, easy to manoeuvre and with enough travel to boost high and land comfortably
                

            

            

            
            
Enduro

            

            

                Enduro, one of the fastest growing styles of mountain biking, it is the most similiar to how leisure mountain bikers ride outside of
                competition. It's all about long days out on the bike in a mountain location. It involves riding up the mountain abd racing down
                technical descents. The bike used in Enduro is a fullsuspension but with less front travel, usually between 120 and 180mm.

The Top 3 Mountain Biking Spots in Ireland


            

              Ireland is known to have some great hot spots for Mountain Biking scattered throughout the country, below we 
              have listed our top 3 spots for you to try!
              

           


           
           
Ticknock

           

           

               Ticknock Mountain Bike Trails are located just 30 mins away from the Dublin City Centre at the Foothills of the Three Rock 
               Mountain.

               

                Ticknock is an amazing place for Mountain Biking, they have all the services you would need on site that you might need to avail
                of. Bike rental, bike repair centre, bike washing, Cafe, tours, lessons and even a shop!

                

                Although, the terrain in Ticknock can prove to be quite technical so some parts of the trails may not be suitable for beginners.
               

           


           
           
Ballinastoe

           

           

               Ballinastoe Trail centre in Wicklow is just 30 minutes out from Dublin. A trip well worth the short road trip!

               

                Ballinastoe is run by the same company as Ticknock and offers the same services. For just 30 Euro you can rent a bike for the
                entire day to explore the trail system.

                

                Ballinastoe has a wide range of trails ranging from small beginners trails to advanced tech trails. Its the perfect centre for
                riders of all capabilities.
               

           


           
           
The GAP

           

           

               The Glencullen Adventure Park is one of the only remaining Bike Parks in Ireland! Located in the outskirts of Dublin.

               

                The GAP is the only Mountain Bike Park in Ireland that offers an official uplift service. This means that a van will 
                drop you off at the top of the mountain to save you from cycling up! Their own slogan is "Lift you up so you can Shred Down!",
                Similiarly to Ticknock and Ballinastoe, they offer a wide range of services. Bike rentals, repair centre, their own restaurant, 
                a shop that provides all sort of biking gear and merch and the uplift service.

                

                The GAP is a gravity park meaning that the trails are all downhill, the skill level of these trails vary from absolute beginner to
                profesional.
